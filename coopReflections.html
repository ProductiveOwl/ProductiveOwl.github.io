Coop Reflections

Hello! Here are my coop term reflections during my time at university. For each 4 month coop term, I have highlighed my goals and accomplishments during my time at each company!

Coop Term 2 - CRA

September - December 2024

About the Company

For my second coop term, I continued to work at the Canada Revenue Agency. As an agency under the federal government of Canada, the CRA is in charge of all taxes and benefits for Canadians.

The CRA’s mission is to “Administer tax, benefits, and related programs, and ensure compliance on behalf of governments across Canada, thereby contributing to the ongoing economic and social well-being of Canadians.”
As this term is a continuation of my previous coop term, I continued working as an IT developer on a front-end application.

Goals

Based on my work and feedback from my last work term, I wanted to improve my knowledge of the CRA systems and my communication skills with other teams. My goals included improving my teamwork, informational literacy, and problem-solving skills.

1) Teamwork - Learning to work on larger issues with multiple people.

During this second work term, I have worked on larger ongoing tasks that involve multiple teams. As our team worked closely with other teams, I learned how to communicate the details we needed to fix our bugs. With help from my team leader, we were able to provide other teams with the information necessary to fix issues on our end. Along with the other IT teams, I also talked to the application users/reporters to investigate the bug. There were a few times when I needed to send follow-up emails and I often received a quick reply. Throughout my term, some projects took longer than expected but I was able to complete most projects I had assigned to me that involved multiple teams. With the spreadsheet I used during my last work term, it was easy to keep track of and follow up with colleagues on what I needed to complete and pending tasks from other teams.

2) Information Literacy - Learn more about how the processes work at CRA.

I have been working on bigger projects which needed me to learn about how our code interacts with other teams. Working on multiple parts of our codebase allowed me to look at various parts of our application and I was assigned to look at a particular function that occurs throughout our program. For 2 months, I inspected the code and any current documentation to record where and how a certain function is invoked. Then, I converted the notes and code into a workflow diagram to document all the decisions that trigger that functionality. This large task improved my ability to analyze code and report the results in a presentable format. Along with this larger task, I have worked on implementing smaller features. This was easier to code and taught me how to update our documentation for future developers. Overall, I have completed this goal as I have learned more about how our application works and read through lots of documentation and code to understand some of the nuances in our application.

3) Problem Solving - Take on more difficult tasks that require further investigation.

Based on the work I have done in my previous term to learn about our systems, I have taken on more complex issues that involve multiple teams and workflows. Although I have been learning a lot about our codebase, working on larger projects took a lot more time than I expected. Compiling the data, analyzing the solution, testing the solution locally, executing the fix and then verifying the fix took longer than necessary with so many scenarios and accounts. I have been using new tools to help me with larger tasks which sometimes involved input from other teams and delayed the investigation process. Using the organization system I set up for myself during my last work term, I kept track of all my notes and the status of each task. This was very helpful when I was working on multiple issues at the same time and when I needed to reference older tickets. With help from my colleagues, I was able to fix more advanced tickets than in my last coop term and I plan to continue improving my problem-solving skills to fix these issues faster.

During my 8-month coop term at the CRA, I continued improving my technical and soft skills as a coder. I learned a lot while working with a large team on a Java Swing application. As I moved to work on more advanced issues, I had a better understanding of the analysis and design that goes into an application with help from my colleagues. Overall, my goals for this semester involved more communication and critical thinking skills which I improved upon during my time at CRA.

Job Description

This term allowed me to work on more intricate parts of our application. I dug deeper through our code to create flowcharts that help us understand how one of our features works. This showed me how useful it is to have documentation and records of our changes. Along with tracing through the logic of our code, I also got to add new features. Adding new labels/indicators allowed users to track their progress easily and who made these changes. I got into some details in our code that interact with other teams and have worked with them to solve overlapping issues. All in all, I got to be a part of our application's design, code and testing portions and have learned a lot about the software development cycle in the industry.

Conclusion & Acknowledgements

Overall, I had an amazing second co-op term at the CRA. Working with an amazing team and team leader, I learned a lot about industry practices and software development. Thanks to my team who were supportive and helped me with the technical and business logic behind the features used in our application! I had a fantastic time at CRA and would love to return someday!

Coop Term 1 - CRA

May - August 2024

About the Company

For my first coop term, I worked at the Canada Revenue Agency. As an agency under the federal government of Canada, the CRA is in charge of all taxes and benefits for Canadians.

The CRA’s mission is to “Administer tax, benefits, and related programs, and ensure compliance on behalf of governments across Canada, thereby contributing to the ongoing economic and social well-being of Canadians.”
As of May 2023, over 62,000 full-time employees were working for the agency across Canada. More specifically, there are 13 headquarters branches and 4 regions that make up the organizational structure of the CRA. One of the headquarters branches is the Informational Technology Branch (ITB) and as of February 2022, there are approximately 4,330 full-time employees. ITB is responsible for providing IT infrastructure for CRA delivery systems with the Finance and Administration Branch along with supporting all CRA employees with the tools they need to help Canadians. I worked in the ITB branch as an IT developer on a front-end application.

Goals

My team works on a Java Swing application and my job involves fixing bugs on the front end. Based on my job, I had the following goals I wanted to accomplish before the end of my work term. My goals included improving my communication skills, organization skills and technological literacy.

1) Oral Communication - Improve my presentation and speaking skills.

To improve my communication skills, I became the scrum leader once a month for a week to keep track of the issues my team was working on. This allowed me to learn about the ongoing projects/issues and the steps we take to resolve them. This was valuable for me as I learned about the various roles my team members have (Specialist, Developer, Tester etc.) and if there are overlapping issues. As everyone on my team works remotely, our daily scrum meetings allow us to update each other about the tasks we are working on and if we need input from other members of the team. Being the scrum leader helped me take on a leadership role for the meeting, interact with all my team members, and write down any updates the team needs to know. Over time, I was able to better communicate with my team as I learned about their tasks and could ask specific and detailed questions about their work.

2) Technological Literacy - Familiarize myself with the debugger tools to investigate my tickets.

For technological literacy, I have learned about the various tools we use at our agency. As my team mainly uses Java, I have used the skills I learned from the OOP course at the University of Guelph and applied those principles when working on tickets. This was also the first time I contributed to a repository with multiple people. This experience improved my knowledge of git and git standards as tracking our changes is important when looking back at the old code. Although I did not work on the back end, I needed to understand how it connects to our front end to describe the issue with the appropriate team. Thus, I learned about how our weblogic server is used in our application as well as some SQL and Cobol. I also became familiar with the Eclipse IDE and the JIRA workflow.

3) Inquiry & Analysis - Establish a routine to diagnose the issue in the ticket.

Lastly, I also wanted to improve my organizational skills. As I had to work on multiple tickets at the same time, I wanted to keep track of my progress on my tickets accurately. I decided to use an Excel spreadsheet and record my progress in multiple places to keep track of my workflow. I used the spreadsheet to keep track of all my current and future tasks and what needed to be accomplished. During the scrum meeting, I would update the team with my current tasks and write down their deadlines. Lastly, at the end of the day, I made a list of all the work I completed and sent it to my team. By recording my tasks in multiple places, it was easy to track my progress and work on multiple issues at the same time.

Overall, I believe that I have accomplished all of these goals as all of the experiences above helped me strengthen my communication, technological literacy, and organizational skills. I hope to continue working on these goals and create new ones based on the new problems I am working on at the CRA for my next work term.

Job Description

My job involves fixing bugs that occur in our Java Swing application. As I have taken the OOP course and created a Java Swing application on my own, I have applied these skills directly to my work at the CRA. Using these skills on a much larger application with many connected components (weblogic, SQL, etc.) helped me learn about how interconnected bigger programs can become. The most interesting part of the project was learning about how our tax application is used and the many nuances that come up when creating a large application. Learning about the backend and how the taxpayer’s information is passed to our applications helped me understand how the consumer uses our program and the impact it has. Thus, coordinating my code changes with other teams, having routine checks, and communicating with clients were an important and interesting part of my job to make sure the application is working correctly. Overall, knowing that these changes will directly affect and help many Canadians makes my job meaningful!

Conclusion

Overall, I enjoyed having my first coop term at the CRA. It was a great opportunity to apply the skills I learned in university to a larger scale application. I have learned many hard skills (such as coding practices, git, using the eclipse IDE, etc.) and improved many soft skills (leading a team meeting, explaining bugs to other teams, organizing and tracking my work, etc.). I hope to continue improving these skills and learning more about the industry during my next work term wtih the CRA!

Acknowledgements

I would like to thank all of my team members that have helped me learn to become a developer at the CRA! They have answered so many of my questions and helped me learn about developer practices. I would also like to thank the CS coop coordinators and the SOCS program counselors for answering all of my questions during my first coop work term!
